In recent years, smartphones have increasingly become part of the educational environment, with many schools adopting policies that allow students to use them in the classroom. Once regarded as a major source of distraction, smartphones are now recognized for their potential to support learning. Schools have started integrating smartphones into lessons, using them for research, communication, and interactive educational tools. This shift is seen as a positive change, as smartphones become valuable resources for students in many classrooms.

Supporters claim that smartphones offer a wide range of educational benefits. They argue that smartphones provide students with access to vast amounts of information and resources on the internet, enabling them to conduct research more efficiently. Smartphones also facilitate communication between students and teachers, making it easier for students to ask questions and engage in discussions. Furthermore, smartphones support interactive learning through educational apps, videos, and games, which enhance student engagement and understanding.

Despite this, critics are concerned about the negative impact of smartphones in the classroom. They argue that smartphones can be distracting, with students more focused on social media or games than on the lesson. There is also concern that smartphones can facilitate cheating during exams or assignments. In contrast, traditional teaching methods, such as using textbooks and face-to-face interactions, are considered more effective for maintaining focus and discipline in the classroom.
